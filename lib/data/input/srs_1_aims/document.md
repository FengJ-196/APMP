2  1   Introduction  1.1   Objective  The primary purpose of this Software Requirement Specification (SRS) is to establish and maintain a formal  agreement between the customers and the developers on what the AIMS (An Internet Media Store) system  should do. This document provides a complete and   unambiguous description of the functional and non -  functional requirements for the AIMS software, which is defined as an e - commerce software (website or  desktop app).  The SRS specifically outlines the requirements for the buying and selling of physical media products,  including books, newspapers, CDs, and DVDs. It serves as a technical foundation for the following intended  audiences:  •   Analysts and Designers: To provide a structural basis for the Software Architecture Design and  Software Detailed Design processes.  •   Developers: To ensure a clear understanding of the system's logic.  •   Testers: To establish qualification requirements and criteria to verify that the software.  •   Project Managers: To provide a basis for estimating cost ,   time and planning the technical contents  of the development iterations.  1.2   Scope  The software product to be produced is AIMS (An Internet Media Store), an e - commerce software available  as either a website or a desktop application.  What the software will do: The system is designed to facilitate the buying and selling of physical media  products, specifically books, newspapers, CDs, and DVDs. Key functionalities include:  •   For Customers: Browsing a 20 - product random list, searching by title or category, filtering by price,  managing a shopping cart, and placing orders with calculated shipping fees.  •   For Product Managers: Adding, editing, and deleting products, manually adjusting stock with  recorded reasons, and reviewing or rejecting pending orders.  •   For Administrators: Managing users (creating, updating, deleting, blocking), resetting passwords,  and assigning multiple roles to users.  •   Payment & Security: Integration of VietQR and PayPal Sandbox for transactions, while maintaining  business rules such as the 30% – 150% price constraint to avoid inflation.  What the software will not do: Currently, AIMS supports only physical media products; other types of  products or digital media services are not supported in the current version. Additionally, while it supports  QR codes and credit cards, other payment metho ds and notification channels (like SMS) are reserved for  future updates.  Objectives and Benefits: The primary goal is to provide a platform that operates 24/7 and allows new users  to easily familiarize themselves. The software is engineered for high performance and reliability, capable of  serving 1,000 customers simultaneously   with a maximum response time of 2   normally   and   up   to 5 seconds  during peak hours . It aims for high availability, operating for 300 hours without failure and ensuring recovery  within 1 hour after any incident

4  2   Overall D escription  2.1   Survey  AIMS is an e - commerce software (available as a website or desktop application) that operates 24/7 to  facilitate the trade of physical media. The system is designed to be user - friendly for new users while  supporting high - concurrency for up to 1,000 simultan eous customers.  The system interacts with the following three primary actors:  •   Customer: An unauthenticated user who interacts with the system to browse a random list of  products, search for items by title or category, and filter results by price. Customers manage their  own shopping carts, provide delivery information, and perform pa yments via QR codes or credit  cards. They can also cancel orders before they are approved by a manager.  •   Product Manager: An internal staff member responsible for the lifecycle of products and orders.  They have the authority to add, edit, or delete products and must manually adjust stock levels for  reasons like damage or new arrivals. Additionally, they are r esponsible for reviewing, approving, or  rejecting pending orders.  •   Administrator: A high - level system user responsible for user management. Their duties include  creating new user accounts, updating user information, resetting passwords, and blocking or  unblocking users as necessary. They also define system access by setti ng or changing user roles,  noting that a single user can hold multiple roles simultaneously.

19  1.   The Product Manager selects at least one and up to ten existing media products from the  product list and requests to delete them.  2.   The AIMS Software verifies the daily deletion limit, ensuring the manager has not  exceeded the maximum of twenty products per day.  3.   For each selected product, the AIMS Software checks the current stock quantity.  4.   The AIMS Software processes the removal based on the following stock conditions:  a.   If stock is equal to zero: The software permanently removes the product record  from the active sales list.  b.   If stock is greater than zero: The software does not delete the product but changes  its status to "deactivated", making it unavailable for sale.  5.   The AIMS Software records the transaction details in the history of products deletion.  6.
